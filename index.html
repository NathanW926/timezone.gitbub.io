World Time Zones

The first time zone starts at longitude zero aka the Greenwich meridian. The meridian got its name from the city Greewich, England; this city is located on latitude zero. How this time map works is every time zone to the left or West of Greenwich will have a negative time, meaning they are what ever hour behind Greenwich. And every time zone to the right or East of Greenwich will have a positve time, meaning they will be however many hours ahead of Greenwich.

Now the world uses Coordinated Universal Time which is the basis for civil time. The UTC was adopted in 1967 and it is adjusted to add in leap seconds to stay in line with the Earth's rotation. Greenwich Mean Time is now a time zone and no longer a time standard. Timeanddate.com states that GMT and UTC share the same current time in practice. Interesting Fun Facts

  • The International Space Station is on the UTC time zone because it's used by scientists.
  • France hsa more time zones than any other country because it has 11 other time zones. This is because France has many territories outside of mainland France.
  • The Railroad is the reason why time zones were invented. To fix complications with resetting watches after traveling to a new location, time zones were created to solve this issue. The U.S.A adopted this idea from a Canadia railroad engineer, Sir Sandford Fleming.
